CIA Triad & AAA

Domain 1 — 12%
CIA Triad
ConfidentialityOnly authorized users can access data
IntegrityData is accurate & unaltered (hashing)
AvailabilitySystems accessible when needed (HA, DR)
AAA Framework
AuthenticationWho are you? (credentials, MFA)
AuthorizationWhat can you do? (ACL, RBAC, ABAC)
AccountingWhat did you do? (logs, audit trails)
Supporting Concepts
Non-repudiationProof of action (digital signatures)
Least PrivilegeMinimum access needed to do the job
Separation of DutiesNo single person controls full process
Defense in DepthLayered controls; no single point of failure
RADIUS & TACACS+ are common AAA protocols. TACACS+ encrypts the full packet; RADIUS only encrypts the password.

Cryptography — Keys & Algorithms

Domain 1 & 3
Symmetric Encryption (same key)
AES-128128-bit key; fast; good for bulk data
AES-192192-bit key; government/sensitive use
AES-256256-bit key; top secret; post-quantum resilient
3DESLegacy; 112-bit effective; being phased out
ChaCha20Stream cipher; fast on mobile hardware
Asymmetric Encryption (key pair)
RSA-2048Public/private; key exchange & signatures
RSA-4096Stronger; higher CPU cost; recommended for new certs
ECC / ECDSAElliptic curve; same strength, shorter keys
Diffie-HellmanKey exchange; never shares the key itself
ECDHElliptic curve DH; provides Perfect Forward Secrecy
Hashing (one-way)
MD5128-bit; broken / collision-vulnerable; legacy only
SHA-1160-bit; deprecated; collision found 2017
SHA-256256-bit; current standard; part of SHA-2 family
SHA-3Keccak sponge construction; distinct from SHA-2
HMACHash + secret key; integrity + authentication
Symmetric = speed (bulk encryption). Asymmetric = key exchange & signatures. Hybrid uses both.

PKI & Certificates

Domain 1 & 3
PKI Components
CA (Root)Certificate Authority; offline root of trust
Intermediate CAIssues end-entity certs; online; limits root exposure
RARegistration Authority; verifies identity before issuance
CRLCertificate Revocation List; periodic batch revocation
OCSPOnline Certificate Status Protocol; real-time check
OCSP StaplingServer caches & serves OCSP response; reduces latency
Certificate Types
DV (Domain Validated)Proves domain control; automated; basic HTTPS
OV (Organization)Org identity verified; shows company in cert
EV (Extended)Strict vetting; green address bar (legacy browsers)
Wildcard*.example.com covers all subdomains (one level)
SANSubject Alt Name; multiple domains on one cert
Self-signedNo CA trust chain; internal use / dev only
TLS Handshake (TLS 1.3)
  1. Client Hello — cipher suites, key share, random nonce
  2. Server Hello — chosen cipher, key share, certificate
  3. Client verifies certificate chain against trusted CA
  4. ECDH key exchange — both derive shared session key
  5. Finished — both send MAC of handshake; encrypted channel open
TLS 1.3 removed RSA key exchange — all sessions use ephemeral DH (PFS). TLS 1.0/1.1 are deprecated.

Incident Response

Domain 4 — PICERL
6 IR Phases (NIST SP 800-61)
1Preparation
2Identification
3Containment
4Eradication
5Recovery
6Lessons Learned
Phase Details
PreparationIRP, playbooks, tools, training, tabletop exercises
IdentificationDetect event; determine if incident; triage severity
ContainmentShort-term (isolate) → long-term (patch/segment)
EradicationRemove malware, close vulnerability, rebuild if needed
RecoveryRestore from clean backup; monitor closely after return
Lessons LearnedPost-incident review; update playbooks; report findings
Digital Forensics Order of Volatility
1. CPU Registers / CacheMost volatile; capture first
2. RAM / Running ProcessesMemory dump before powering off
3. Network ConnectionsActive sessions, routing tables
4. Disk / StorageBit-for-bit image; preserve chain of custody
5. Backups / Remote LogsLeast volatile; SIEM archives
Chain of custody: document who handled evidence, when, and how — required for legal proceedings.
IR Team Roles
CIRT / CSIRTComputer Incident Response Team; leads response
SOC AnalystTier 1 triage → Tier 2 investigation → Tier 3 threat hunt
Threat HunterProactively seeks hidden attackers (assume breach)

Risk Management

Domain 5 — 20%
Risk Fundamentals
Risk = Threat × Vulnerability × ImpactCore equation
Inherent RiskRisk before any controls applied
Residual RiskRisk remaining after controls; accepted or transferred
Risk RegisterDocument of identified risks, owners, scores, treatment
Risk Treatment Options
AvoidEliminate the activity causing the risk
TransferCyber insurance; contractual liability shift
MitigateApply controls to reduce likelihood or impact
AcceptResidual risk within tolerance; document decision
Quantitative vs Qualitative
QuantitativeFinancial values: ALE = ARO × SLE; hard numbers
SLESingle Loss Expectancy = Asset Value × EF
AROAnnual Rate of Occurrence; how often per year
ALEAnnual Loss Expectancy = SLE × ARO
QualitativeLikelihood × Impact matrix; High/Med/Low ratings
BIA & Recovery Metrics
BIABusiness Impact Analysis; identifies critical processes
RTORecovery Time Objective; max tolerable downtime
RPORecovery Point Objective; max tolerable data loss
MTTRMean Time To Repair; avg time to fix a failure
MTBFMean Time Between Failures; avg operational lifespan
RTO < RPO is rare; they define your DR tier: Hot (minutes), Warm (hours), Cold (days).
